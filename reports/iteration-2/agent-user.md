## OVERALL DROP-OFF RISK

**Highest risk: Budget Setup (Setup Step 6)**

This is where most users will quit. After 5 steps of smooth onboarding, you hit them with 14 empty fields and ask them to know their monthly spend by category. Most South Africans don't budget formally. They know "groceries are expensive" but not "R3,200/month."

The skip button saves some users, but they leave feeling like they failed.

**Second highest: Home screen (first visit, zero expenses)**

If Tjommie's first message doesn't give a CLEAR, SPECIFIC next action ("Tap the + button and add your phone contract"), users will wander the tabs, feel overwhelmed by empty states, and close the app.

---

## TOP 3 FIXES

### 1. Budget Setup: Default to the Big 3 only
Change Step 6 to ask ONLY for:
- Housing (Rent/Bond)
- Groceries & Food
- Transport (Fuel/Taxi)

Pre-fill with suggested amounts based on income. Add a toggle: "Add more categories (optional)". This drops friction from 4/5 to 2/5 and keeps users in the flow.

### 2. Home Screen First Visit: Direct the user explicitly
First-time Tjommie message: "Right [name], let's log your first work expense. Tap the gold + button at the bottom." Add a pulsing animation to the FAB. This eliminates the "now what?" paralysis.

### 3. Add Expense: Remove category-first, go amount-first
Despite the build instructions, users think about AMOUNT first when looking at a receipt ("I just spent R450"), then remember what it was for. Swap the order:
1. Amount (with live "Adds R120 to your 13th Cheque" preview)
2. Category (now you care what to call it)
3. Description (optional)

This matches user mental model and reduces hesitation.

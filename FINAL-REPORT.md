# Final Report

The 13th Cheque final build is a 3,400-line single-file React prototype that takes the v2 starter — which had basic tax math, generic budget categories, and a simple onboarding flow — and turns it into a production-shaped South African tax refund tracker with a full SARS 2025/26 compliant engine, eight-step onboarding, and contextual AI chat. The three most important improvements over v2 are: **(1) A real tax calculation engine** — v2 only computed income tax and marginal savings, while the final build adds Retirement Annuity deductions (27.5% of income, R350k cap), medical tax credits (Section 6A, per-member and per-dependant), age-based rebates (primary/secondary/tertiary), and Section 23(m) enforcement that restricts deduction categories for salaried employees in the UI rather than just mentioning the rule in a chat prompt. **(2) Career-aware personalised onboarding** — after the user enters their occupation, Tjommie surfaces a pre-selected shortlist of deductions specific to their role (nine career profiles from teacher to driver), so users confirm rather than guess, which is the difference between a tax app people use and one they abandon. **(3) South African household budget categories with formatted currency input** — v2 had seven generic categories with hardcoded limits; the final build has 14 SA-specific categories (housing, utilities, groceries, transport, family support, airtime & data, etc.) each with dropdown sub-items, plus a formatted R-value currency input component that replaces the raw number field, making the core data entry experience feel like a fintech product rather than a government form.
